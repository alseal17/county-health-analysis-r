Project Overview

This project analyzes county-level public health data to explore how socioeconomic, behavioral, healthcare access, and environmental factors relate to life expectancy across U.S. counties.

Research Questions

  1. How does median household income relate to life expectancy?
  2. How does education relate to life expectancy?
  3. How are smoking, obesity, poverty, and healthcare access associated with life expectancy?
  4. Which public health indicators show the strongest relationships with life expectancy?

Conclusion

This project demonstrates how R can be used to clean, explore, visualize, and model public health data. Overall, the analysis suggests that life expectancy across U.S. counties is associated with a combination of socioeconomic conditions, health behaviors, and healthcare access factors. Higher income and education levels generally appear related to longer life expectancy, while smoking, obesity, poverty, and uninsured rates appear related to poorer health outcomes.

This project is exploratory and does not prove causation, but it provides a useful example of how county-level public health data can be analyzed to identify patterns and communicate findings clearly.
